T21 Conference

Fall 2012

T21 is an International Conference on Cutural Tolerance in the 21st Century. The aim of the T21 Conference is to underline the importance of human rights, fundamental freedoms and democratic institutions in creating a context for inter-cultural, inter-religious and inter-ethnic understanding.

I was given the task to prototype an identity for this conference designing everything from the poster to the signage used throughout the event. I developed my concept around bringing awareness to the tension caused by intolerant behavior. I represented this idea by creating an eye out of black & white lines, particularly focusing on where the lines collided. This later became the theme I used for the rest of the conference.

After Your Last Cigarette

Fall 2012

When asked to complete an information graphic regarding a health problem, I chose to confront an affliction millions suffer from. Smoking and its addiction causes many health problems ranging anywhere from cancer to heart disease.

What I wanted to do with this problem was advertise quitting in a positive light. I wanted to show people who smoke that there are benefits to breaking the habit.

I did so by creating a motion graphic animation, illustrating how the body repairs itself immediately following the act of quitting.

Cue Magazine

Spring 2012

The objective was to design and brand a prototype magazine of any subject matter. I chose to do a monthly film magazine.

Cue magazine would include: an in depth analysis of an upcoming film, a movie poster/synopsis spread of newly released films, a title sequence breakdown, & articles involving the movie industry. In this particular issue, I included Prometheus, The Girl with the Dragon Tattoo, & an article from Roger Ebert regarding the changing movie industry.

The magazine is perfect bound, typeset in Bembo Standard & Akzidenz-Grotesk BQ, and printed on Mohawk glossy paper.

Curator

Winter 2011

In order to develop Curator I had to first confront the task which involved designing a service that organized and maintained the art collection at the Isabella Stewart Gardner Museum in Boston, MA.

I came up with the idea of a to-do list after attending the museum and noticing that it's layout, although beautiful isn't exactly intuitive. Curator would act as an organized guide for the museum visitor, making sure they can view, save, queue, annotate & locate they're desired art pieces from any museum. The provided video walk-through displays Curator's user interface and as well as some basic functions.

Information Graphics

Fall 2011

Infographics are visual representations of information. My goal as a designer is to make this information not only clear and accessible, but also pleasant to look at.

"100 Bands I've Seen Live" is a graph I made that represents the number of concerts I've seen over the years. It also includes the artist names, genre, year attended, whether I continue to listen to their music or not & what bands played together at each concert.

The next graphic, "Origins" features Girl Talk's track, "No Pause." The objective here was to design a printed diagram that clearly and understandably conveys the organization and structure of a complex musical composition. I displayed the song by tracking the origin of each musician he samples.
